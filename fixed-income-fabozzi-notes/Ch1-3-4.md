## 🧱 1. Bond = Cashflow Object

A bond is a deterministic or semi-deterministic stream of future cashflows:

- Cashflows = Coupons + Principal
- Defined by:
  - Par Value (Face Value)
  - Coupon Rate (fixed or floating)
  - Maturity (time horizon)

👉 Core abstraction:
Bond = {Cashflows, Timing, Optionality}

---

## ⚙️ 2. Cashflow Complexity (Important for Pricing)

### Simple Bonds
- Fixed cashflows → deterministic pricing

### Complex Bonds
- Floating Rate Bonds → cashflows depend on reference rates
- Callable Bonds → issuer has option to repay early
- MBS/ABS/CDO → cashflows depend on underlying asset behavior

👉 Key Insight:
More uncertainty in cashflows → harder pricing → requires models

---

## 🔁 3. Optionality (CRITICAL)

Embedded options change cashflow structure:

- Callable Bonds → issuer benefits when rates fall
- MBS → borrower prepayment option
- Sinking Fund → partial early redemption

👉 Quant Interpretation:
Bond = Cashflows + Embedded Options

---

## 📉 4. Negative Convexity

Occurs in:
- Callable bonds
- Mortgage-backed securities (MBS)

👉 Behavior:
- When rates ↓ → price increases less than expected
- Due to early repayment risk

---

## 🏦 5. Markets = Where Prices (Yields) Come From

### Primary Market
- New bonds issued
- Initial pricing set

### Secondary Market (IMPORTANT)
- Bonds trade continuously
- Market determines:
  - Price
  - Yield

👉 Key Insight:
Yields are **observed from market prices**, not assumed

---

## 🧾 6. Market Structure

- Bonds trade Over-The-Counter (OTC)
- Dealer-based system (no centralized exchange)

👉 Implications:
- Prices vary across dealers
- Liquidity matters

---

## 💸 7. Bid-Ask Spread

- Dealers provide liquidity
- Spread = cost of immediacy

👉 Components (intuition only):
- Transaction cost
- Inventory risk
- Price uncertainty

---

## 📊 8. Bond Index = Portfolio Object

- Index = weighted portfolio of bonds
- Typically market-value weighted

👉 Quant Interpretation:
Index has:
- Yield
- Duration
- Risk exposure

---

## 🔗 9. Correlation Structure (VERY IMPORTANT)

- Investment-grade bonds highly correlated
- Driven by:
  👉 Yield curve movements

👉 Key Insight:
Interest rates = dominant risk factor

---

## 📈 10. Core Pricing Inputs (Preview)

### Spot Rate
- Discount rate for a single cashflow

### Forward Rate
- Implied future interest rate

👉 These will be used for:
- Yield curve construction
- Advanced pricing

---

## 💰 11. Price Definitions

### Dirty Price (Full Price)
= Clean Price + Accrued Interest

👉 Used in actual transactions

---

## ⚠️ 12. Market Reality (Important for Quants)

- Not all bonds trade frequently
- Prices may be estimated (matrix pricing)
- Liquidity affects observed prices

---

## 🧠 FINAL MENTAL MODEL

A bond is:

→ A stream of cashflows  
→ Possibly uncertain (due to optionality)  
→ Discounted using market-implied rates  
→ Traded in a liquidity-driven OTC market  

---

## 🔥 ONE-LINE SUMMARY

Bond = Discounted Cashflows + Optionality + Market-Implied Rates
